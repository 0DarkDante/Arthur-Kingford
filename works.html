WORKS

A pivotal moment in Arthur’s artistic career came at the age of 17 when he won the Grand Prix at a national music competition in Ukraine. This victory marked the turning point from which his professional journey as a performer truly began.

In 2020, Arthur competed at the International Open Trophy, sharing the stage in the same category with his childhood idol – an experience that became both inspiring and formative. Two years later, in 2022, he achieved another milestone by entering the Top 20 accordionists worldwide at the prestigious Trophée Mondial de l’Accordéon.

Alongside his concert career, Arthur is actively engaged in research. His academic work explores musical heritage and performance practices, contributing to the preservation and development of cultural traditions. Research works include:

  • Alexander Technique: Fundamentals and Influence on the Performance of Instrumental Musicians
  • The Phenomenon of Volodymyr Lutsiv’s Creativity in the Context of Ukrainian Cultural Processes of the 20th–21st Centuries

As an educator, Arthur has conducted over 2,500 private lessons, mentoring students of various levels and backgrounds. His teaching combines technical mastery with artistic individuality, nurturing the next generation of musicians.

Composition holds a significant place in his creative path. Each of Arthur’s works is a personal reflection, combining emotional depth with innovative approaches. His notable pieces include:

  • Glacier
  • Gentle Sparks
  • Heritage
  • Shadow
  • Elysium

Arthur Kingford continues to expand his artistic vision, taking part in cultural projects, developing new repertoire, and enriching the musical landscape as a performer, composer, and educator.
